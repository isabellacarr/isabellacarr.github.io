Hello, my name is Isabella Carranza and I am a second-year undergraduate student at the University of California Riverside studying psychology. Although psychology is my major I am also interested in law and society, so in the future, I hope to work in the area of criminal justice and law enforcement. To get to know me better I'll share a few things about myself. I am currently a student-athlete, running on the cross-country and track teams at UCR. I have two dogs and in my free time, I enjoy reading and watching movies.

As I continue to learn more about psychology I have also learned more about our criminal justice system in the United States. Over the summer of 2022, I completed an internship with the non-profit organization Inside Circle. I listened to the organization's podcast episodes and pulled out information that was then put into a boilerplate that was used to further support their work. Over the course of this internship, I learned a lot about the importance of having counseling services for the people inside prisons. This internship gave me a better understanding of the criminal justice system in the United States and the amount of reform it continues to need. I hope one day to use my psychology degree to help make a difference in the criminal justice system.

Growing up I have also helped volunteer at my church. On Saturday mornings I would volunteer at the food pantry. I would help pass out food, restock shelves, and help people take groceries to their cars. Throughout my years in high school, I also volunteered at an animal shelter. This task included walking the dogs and cleaning up the facility. Lastly, during the summer and springtime, I would volunteer at the elementary school where my Aunt worked. I really enjoyed this experience because I realized that I really liked working with children. Helping the students read, write, color, or paint made me happy. It made me consider working with children in the future. Overall, my past experiences have helped me understand how help is really needed in all parts of the world. It also taught me important skills like the importance of time management and communication.
